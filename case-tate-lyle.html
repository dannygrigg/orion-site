Case study — Food manufacturing

Tate & Lyle automates with bespoke food-grade sugar line conveyors

Orion implemented a pick & pack solution handling up to 400 units per hour — with over 50% of materials sourced from recycled content.

24/7
Continuous operation required
0
Permitted downtime

Engineering challenges overcome

Overview

The customer needed to increase throughput and quality of their existing system to keep up with client demand. Orion implemented a pick & pack solution able to handle client orders of up to 400 units per hour. Notably, over 50% of the solution is made from recycled materials, providing significant cost savings for the client.

Challenges

  1. Food grade washdown requirements
  2. Handling extremely volatile and abrasive materials (sugar)
  3. 24/7 running — zero downtime permitted
  4. Quick-change components required for rapid maintenance
  5. Awkward product media requiring bespoke solutions
  6. Existing controls architecture to integrate with
  7. Routing through existing equipment with precise handshake

Solutions

  1. Bespoke stainless-steel conveyor construction throughout
  2. Plastic chain technology for food-safe handling of abrasive materials
  3. Quick-change infill sections for rapid maintenance without specialist tools
  4. Plug-and-play motors designed for removal with basic tools
  5. Enhanced access and service points throughout the system
  6. Extended FAT testing to ensure 24/7 reliability before deployment
  7. Out-of-hours redeployment to site — minimal disruption to operations
"We recently partnered with Orion to address our unique needs for a bespoke food-grade conveyor system, and the results have been nothing short of exceptional. The bespoke conveyor, designed with meticulous attention to detail and in compliance with the highest food safety standards, has proven to be a game-changer for our operations. Impressed by the success of this collaboration, we are excited to announce that we will be rolling out Orion's bespoke conveyor systems across all our domestic and international sites."
