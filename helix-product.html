How it works

Sorting in three steps,
at pace

The Helix system combines induction, vision, and high-speed swivel wheel sortation into a compact, modular platform that fits inside your existing building.

01
Induction & singulation
Parcels enter via the loading conveyor and are singulated — separated into a single stream with controlled gap spacing. Vision cameras read barcodes, capture dimensions, and assign each parcel to a sort destination in real time.
02
Swivel wheel divert
Each Helix module uses swivel wheel technology to divert parcels left, right, or straight at up to 2.5 m/s. Modules are 300mm in pitch — the more modules per Helix, the longer the parcel it can handle reliably.
03
Chute outfeed
Diverted parcels travel down powered or non-powered chute conveyors to their destination lane. Each Helix unit handles two sort destinations — left and right — allowing compact system layouts with high destination counts.

Engineering detail

Why every decision matters

The Helix isn't a catalogue product with a new badge. Every dimension, material, and mechanism has been chosen for parcel sortation specifically.

01 — Module design
300mm pitch isn't arbitrary
The 300mm module pitch was chosen to balance parcel support area against system compactness. Shorter modules would require more units for long parcels. Longer modules would waste footprint on small parcels. 300mm is the engineering optimum for mixed parcel profiles.
02 — Divert mechanism
Swivel wheel: speed without shock
Swivel wheel diverts steer parcels rather than pushing them. This means no impact force on the parcel, no jams from friction, and consistent divert accuracy at full speed. At 2,500 mm/s, that matters enormously for sort accuracy and machine life.
03 — Cassette construction
15-minute MTTR by design, not chance
Each Helix module is a self-contained cassette. Bearing, wheel assembly, and drive are all accessible without tools from the top of the machine. No need to stop the whole system — a module swaps in 15 minutes while adjacent modules continue running.
04 — UK manufacture
87% in-house isn't a marketing claim
87% of Helix components are manufactured at Orion's UK facility. This means we control tolerances, we control lead times, and we control the supply chain. When a customer needs a part urgently, we don't wait for a container from overseas.
